What are CIDR and subnet masks?

CIDR stands for Classless Inter-Domain Routing. It is a method for allocating IP addresses and routing Internet Protocol packets. It is a replacement for the previous classful network architecture, which used the classes A, B, and C to define networks based on their size.

How to calculate the CIDR of a subnet mask?

To calculate the CIDR of a subnet mask, count the number of 1 bits in the subnet mask. For example, when the subnet mask 255.255.255.224 is converted into binary, is 11111111.11111111.11111111.11100000. There are 27 1 bits, and therefore the CIDR is /27.

How to calculate the subnet mask of a CIDR?

As stated above, you can get the CIDR by counting the 1 bits of the subnet mask. With this logic, you can get the subnet mask by setting the bits to 1 from left to right, and then converting each octet into its decimal form.
